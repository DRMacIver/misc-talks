EMOTIONS

What's up with those?

The theory in a nutshell

  1. An emotion word labels behaviour, not internal state.
  2. Feeling an emotion is some part of you wanting to behave that way.
  3. It wants this because it thinks that behaviour is a good idea.
  4. It may or may not be right.
  5. When it is not, you can often change the feeling.
  6. When it is, you can learn to act skillfully on it.

EMOTIONS LABEL BEHAVIOUR

  • You can tell because there are a lot of words for them.
  • ...and also because they're clearly made up (but still describe real things)
  • Useful motto: Emotions are like colours.

YOU (LITERALLY) FEEL EMOTIONS

  • "Feelings" are primarily actual physical feelings.
  • It can be a bit subtle and not always useful to think of them this way, but it's a good starting point.
  • The sense you use to access them is called "interoception".
  • When you feel an emotion there is a literal physical change that you can notice.
  • Improving your emotional awareness is "just" a matter of paying attention to that.

WHAT FEELS TRUE?

  • When you say or hear something false, notice what that feels like.
  • Particularly your instantaneous reaction.
  • Learn to go "Hmm no that's quite right... It's more like..."

WORKING WITH PARTS

  • "Part of me believes..."
  • Useful to think of parts as people in their own right.
  • This isn't literally true.
  • (Probably)
  • "I'm a different person when..."

EMOTIONS AS STRATEGIC CHOICES

  • Feeling an emotion is a part wanting to behave that way.
  • It wants this because it believes it's a good idea.
  • It's learned that from experience.
  • That doesn't mean it's right!
  • But you should probably take it seriously.

IMPROVING BELIEFS

  • Ask "Why do I want to do this?"
  • Ask "What does this remind me of?" or "Where did I learn this behaviour?"
  • Ask "Is this situation different in important ways?"
  • (This is all much easier to do before of after a triggering event)

ACTING ON BELIEFS

  • Ask "What do I want to do?"
  • Ask "What would make this feel safer?"
  • Come up with a plan.

DON'T...

  • Treat your emotions as automatically wrong.
  • Treat your emotions as automatically right.
  • Go in refusing to act on an emotion.
  • Feel like you have to act on an emotion in the first way that comes to mind.
  • Expect to get all of this right all the time.

DO...

  • Treat your parts with respect.
  • Think of your emotions as signal not noise.
  • Learn to act on them.
  • Learn to notice and correct when they are wrong.
